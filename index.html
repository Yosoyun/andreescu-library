Few people have shaped the mathematical lives of more students than Titu Andreescu. Across four decades — first in Romania, then in the United States and, through his books, in classrooms from Chennai to California — he has done one thing better than almost anyone alive: he has taught people how to think about hard problems.

He coached the USA team that scored a perfect 252 at the 1994 International Mathematical Olympiad. He directed America's competitions, founded the AwesomeMath summer program, launched the free journal Mathematical Reflections, and built XYZ Press into a home for serious problem books. And he has authored, co-authored, or edited more than fifty of them.

Yet many talented students — and even teachers — have never heard his name. This library exists to change that. Everything below is organised so that a curious student in any town can discover his work, understand why it matters, and find a legitimate way to read it.

The Life

✦

A Teacher's Journey

From a prize-winning schoolboy in Timișoara to one of the most prolific authors in olympiad mathematics.

1956
Born in Timișoara, Romania; encouraged toward mathematics early by his father and uncle.
1973–75
Wins the Romanian national problem-solving contests three years running as a high-school student.
1980s
Teaches at the Constantin Diaconovici Loga school, edits Revista Matematică din Timișoara, and coaches the Romanian IMO team. Named "Distinguished Professor" (1983).
1990
Emigrates to the United States; joins the Illinois Mathematics and Science Academy.
1994
Coaches the USA IMO team to a perfect score in Hong Kong — the first time any nation achieved this.
1998–2003
Directs the Mathematical Association of America's American Mathematics Competitions (AMC); serves as head coach of the USA IMO team and chairman of the USAMO.
2002
Elected to the IMO Advisory Board.
2006
Founds AwesomeMath (summer program), the free journal Mathematical Reflections, and the Metroplex Math Circle. Becomes a professor at the University of Texas at Dallas.
2003 →
Earns his Ph.D. (Diophantine analysis) and builds XYZ Press into a leading home for olympiad problem books — authoring dozens across every subject.

The Legacy

What He Built

Andreescu's influence is not only in his books — it is in the institutions that now train the next generation.

Since 2006

AwesomeMath

A summer program for gifted middle- and high-school students, expanded to UT Dallas, Cornell, and the University of Puget Sound — plus a year-round program (AMY).

Since 2006 · Free

Mathematical Reflections

A free online journal he founded — six issues a year of problems and articles for students and teachers. Every issue is free to read.

1998–2003

The AMC

As director of the MAA's American Mathematics Competitions, he shaped the contests that identify and develop talent across the United States.

Publisher

XYZ Press

The publishing house he built specifically for rigorous, beautiful problem-solving books — many of which appear in the catalogue below.

10 Years

USA IMO Team

Head coach of the United States team (1993–2002) and director of the Mathematical Olympiad Summer Program — including the historic 1994 perfect score.

Since 2006

Metroplex Math Circle

A community math circle at UT Dallas bringing olympiad-style enrichment to local students every weekend.

Why This Matters — and How to Use It

If you are a student

  • Start free. Read a few problems in Mathematical Reflections and try the AMC archive. Cost nothing, lose nothing.
  • Pick your level. Use the filters above — begin with Intermediate titles, grow into Olympiad, dream toward Problems from the Book.
  • Choose by weakness. Bad at geometry? Filter the topic and work one book cover to cover.
  • You don't need a coach to start. These books are the coaching — that was always the point.

If you are a teacher

  • Share this page. One link gives your whole class a map of where to go next.
  • Build a reading ladder. Sequence titles by level for a year-long enrichment track.
  • Use the free journal as a steady supply of fresh, vetted problems for clubs and circles.
  • Order for your library. The library/WorldCat links make it easy to request institutional copies.
