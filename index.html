The Life

From a prize-winning schoolboy in Timișoara to one of the most prolific authors in olympiad mathematics.

b. 19 Aug 1956Timișoara, RomaniaUT DallasPh.D. 2003

Few people have shaped the mathematical lives of more students than Titu Andreescu. Across four decades — first in Romania, then in the United States, and through his books in classrooms from Chennai to California — he has done one thing better than almost anyone alive: he has taught people how to think about hard problems.

Born in Timișoara in 1956, he won the Romanian national problem-solving contests three years running as a schoolboy, then coached the Romanian IMO team through the 1980s. After emigrating to the United States in 1990, he coached the USA IMO team to a perfect score in 1994 — the first time any nation achieved it — directed America's competitions, and earned his Ph.D. on Diophantine analysis.

In 2006 he founded AwesomeMath, the free journal Mathematical Reflections, and the Metroplex Math Circle, and built XYZ Press into a home for serious problem books. He has authored, co-authored, or edited more than forty distinct books — far more counting editions and the annual and journal volumes — yet many talented students have never heard his name. This library exists to change that.

Why This Matters — and How to Use It

If you are a student

  • Start free. Read a few problems in Mathematical Reflections and try the AMC archive. Cost nothing, lose nothing.
  • Pick your level. Use the filters — begin with Intermediate titles, grow into Olympiad, dream toward Problems from the Book.
  • Choose by weakness. Bad at geometry? Filter the topic and work one book cover to cover.
  • You don't need a coach to start. These books are the coaching — that was always the point.

If you are a teacher

  • Share this page. One link gives your whole class a map of where to go next.
  • Build a reading ladder. Sequence titles by level for a year-long enrichment track.
  • Use the free journal as a steady supply of fresh, vetted problems for clubs and circles.
  • Order for your library. The library/WorldCat links make institutional copies easy to request.
